## Part 0 — Before You Start: Key Concepts

### Employment Structure (מבנה העסקה)

A foreign caregiver in Israel is employed at a **24/6 full-time scope** (היקף משרה 24/6) — meaning 6 days a week, 24 hours a day at the patient's home. The Supreme Court (בג"ץ) has ruled that the Working Hours and Rest Law (חוק שעות עבודה ומנוחה) does **not** apply to live-in caregivers due to the nature of their residency at the workplace. There is therefore **no overtime pay** for live-in caregivers.

### Inputs You Need Before Calculating

To produce an accurate monthly pay stub, gather the following:

| Input | Hebrew | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| Month/year of the stub | חודש ושנת השכר | Determines applicable minimum wage |
| Days the employee was present | ימי עבודה בפועל | All days in the home, including rest day if worked |
| Weekly rest day worked? | עבד/ה ביום המנוחה? | Each rest day worked triggers 150% premium |
| Holiday days (out of the 9 chosen) | ימי חג שנעדר/עבד | From the list signed at contract start |
| Vacation days taken (paid) | ימי חופשה שנוצלו | From accrued balance |
| Sick days taken | ימי מחלה | With medical certificate (אישור רפואי) |
| Seniority (months employed) | ותק בחודשים | Needed for pension, severance, vacation accrual |
| Pocket money paid weekly | דמי כיס ששולמו | ₪100/week if written into the contract |
| Actual medical insurance cost | עלות ביטוח רפואי בפועל | Needed to calculate the deductible half |
| Accommodation type | סוג מגורים | Employer-owned vs. rented (affects deduction ceiling) |
| Food deduction agreed? | ניכוי כלכלה מוסכם? | Must be in writing; up to 10% of salary |

## Part 1 — Establish the Base Gross Salary (שכר יסוד ברוטו)

### Step 1.1 — Set the Minimum Wage Floor (שכר מינימום)

The employee's monthly gross salary **cannot fall below the legal minimum wage**. As of **April 1, 2026**:

| Unit | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Monthly (חודשי) | **₪6,443.85** |
| Daily (יומי) | **₪257.75** (= 6,443.85 ÷ 25) |
| Hourly (שעתי) | **₪35.40** |

> The divisor of **25** is the standard legal working-day divisor for a 6-day-week employee (25 working days per month on average).

If the employer agreed to pay more than minimum wage in the employment contract, use that higher figure as the base. All social benefits are calculated on **whichever is higher**: the actual base or minimum wage.

### Step 1.2 — Include Pocket Money (דמי כיס)

If pocket money (דמי כיס) was written into the employment contract:

- Standard amount: **₪100 per weekend** rest day (regardless of whether it is taken inside or outside the home).
- These are a **salary advance** (מקדמה על חשבון השכר) — they are part of the monthly salary and must appear on the stub as such.
- Months with 4 weekends: ₪400 advance; months with 5 weekends: ₪500 advance.
- At month-end, the remaining balance owed = Base Salary − Total Pocket Money Paid.

> There is **no legal obligation** to pay pocket money unless it was agreed in the contract. But once written in, it is binding.

## Part 2 — Add Premium Pay for Worked Rest Days (תוספת יום מנוחה)

### Step 2.1 — Identify the Rest Day (יום המנוחה)

The weekly rest day is **25 consecutive hours**, falling on Friday, Saturday, or Sunday — according to the employee's religion/custom, as agreed in the contract.

### Step 2.2 — Calculate Premium if Rest Day Was Worked (גמול יום מנוחה)

If the employee **worked on their designated rest day** (only with their consent and employer need), they are entitled to a premium:

- **Payment = 150% of the daily rate**, applied to the full 25-hour rest day.
- As of April 2026: **₪426.35 per rest day worked** (based on ₪257.75 × 150% ÷ 24 × 25, or approximately the stated floor).

> This premium is **per worked rest day**, not per hour. If the employee worked 3 rest days in a month, multiply by 3.

**Formula:**
```
Rest Day Premium = Number of Worked Rest Days × ₪426.35 (minimum floor)
```

---

## Part 3 — Handle Holiday Days (ימי חג)

### Step 3.1 — The 9 Chosen Holidays (ימי חג נבחרים)

At the start of employment, the employee chooses **9 holidays per year** from either Israeli holidays or their own religion/country's holidays. This list is fixed for the employment period and written into the contract.

### Step 3.2 — Employee Did Not Work on a Holiday

If a holiday from the employee's chosen list fell within the month and the employee **did not work**:

- The employee receives their **full regular monthly salary** as if they worked — no deduction is made.
- This must appear on the stub as a line item: "Holiday Pay" (דמי חג).
- Do **not** defer holiday pay to year-end — doing so constitutes wage withholding (הלנת שכר).

### Step 3.3 — Employee Worked on a Holiday

If the employee agreed to work on a holiday:

- They are entitled to **150% of the daily rate** (same calculation as a rest day).
- Minimum: **₪426.35** per holiday worked.
- If the holiday falls on the same day as the weekly rest day, **do not pay double** — pay once.

---

## Part 4 — Handle Vacation Days (ימי חופשה שנתית)

### Step 4.1 — Annual Vacation Entitlement (זכאות לחופשה שנתית)

| Seniority (ותק) | Annual Days |
|-----------------|-------------|
| Years 1–5 | 14 days/year |
| Year 6 | 16 days/year |
| Year 7 | 18 days/year |
| Each additional year | +1 day |

Monthly accrual rate: Annual entitlement ÷ 12.

### Step 4.2 — Calculating Vacation Pay (תשלום חופשה)

Vacation days are paid at the **regular daily rate**:

```
Vacation Day Value = Monthly Salary ÷ 25 = ₪257.75/day (at minimum wage)
```

If the employee took paid vacation days this month:
- The salary remains full (they are "in" the salary).
- Record the number of days used and deduct from the accrued vacation balance.
- **Do not pay extra** — vacation days are already covered by the regular monthly salary.

### Step 4.3 — Unused Vacation Balance

Maintain a running vacation balance on each pay stub (ויתרת ימי חופשה). Unused days can accumulate and must be paid out in cash upon termination.

---

## Part 5 — Handle Sick Days (ימי מחלה)

### Step 5.1 — Accrual Rate (צבירת ימי מחלה)

- **1.5 sick days per month worked**, up to **18 days per year**.
- Maximum cumulative balance: **90 sick days**.
- Valid only with a **doctor's certificate** (אישור רפואי) for each absence.

### Step 5.2 — Payment Rules (תשלום ימי מחלה)

| Sick Day Number | Payment |
|-----------------|---------|
| Day 1 (יום ראשון) | **Unpaid (0%)** |
| Days 2–3 (ימים ב'–ג') | **50% of regular daily wage** |
| Day 4 onwards | **100% of regular daily wage** |

**Formula per sick absence:**
```
Day 1:  ₪0
Day 2:  ₪257.75 × 50% = ₪128.88
Day 3:  ₪257.75 × 50% = ₪128.88
Day 4+: ₪257.75 × 100% each
```

> Important: If the employee was not absent (i.e., sick days overlap with rest days or holidays), those days do not count against the sick day quota.

---

## Part 6 — Recovery Pay (דמי הבראה)

### Step 6.1 — Entitlement Start (תחילת זכאות)

The employee becomes entitled to recovery pay **after completing 1 full year** with the same employer.

### Step 6.2 — Rate and Scale (תעריף ומדרגות)

| Seniority | Days per Year | Value per Day | Annual Total |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|--------------|
| Year 1 | 5 days | ₪418 | ₪2,090 |
| Years 2–3 | 6 days | ₪418 | ₪2,508 |
| Years 4–10 | 7 days | ₪418 | ₪2,926 |

> The ₪418/day rate is current as of 2024–2026 (private sector). It is updated annually by the Ministry of Finance.

### Step 6.3 — When to Pay (מועד תשלום)

- Typically paid annually, during **June–September** (the Israeli holiday/summer season).
- The employer and employee may agree to **spread it monthly** — if so, it must appear as a **separate line** on every pay stub, not merged with base salary.
- Recovery pay accrues proportionally — if only 7 months of a year were worked, pay 7/12 of the annual entitlement.
- Recovery pay is **separate from and in addition to** minimum wage.

## Part 7 — Permitted Deductions (ניכויים מותרים)

> ⚠️ All deductions require **written consent** from the employee in the employment contract. Unwritten deductions are illegal and can result in fines. Even if deductions are made, all **social benefits** (pension, severance, vacation, sick pay) must be calculated on the **full minimum wage**, not on the post-deduction net.

### Step 7.1 — Medical Insurance Deduction (ניכוי ביטוח רפואי)

The employer is **legally required** to purchase private medical insurance for the foreign employee. The employer may deduct from the employee's salary:

- Up to **half (50%) of the actual insurance cost paid**, AND
- Not more than **₪164.91/month** (ceiling as of 2025–2026, per PIBA Circular 12/2025).

**Formula:**
```
Medical Deduction = min(Actual Cost ÷ 2, ₪164.91)
```

### Step 7.2 — Accommodation Deduction (ניכוי מגורים)

The employer provides accommodation in their home. They may deduct for housing costs. The ceiling depends on the accommodation type:

**When accommodation is employer-owned (in the patient's home):**
The deduction ceiling varies by geographic area (מחוז) as set annually by PIBA. For a caregiver living in the employer's home, the maximum in most areas is several hundred shekels per month — verify the exact current regional table from the PIBA annual circular.

### Step 7.3 — Incidental Accommodation Costs (הוצאות נלוות למגורים)

In addition to the base accommodation deduction, the employer may deduct for utilities (electricity, water, municipal tax):

- 2026 ceiling: **₪94.34/month**
- 2025 ceiling was ₪92.16/month; 2024 was ₪89.11/month.

### Step 7.4 — Food Deduction (ניכוי כלכלה)

If written into the contract and the employee consented:

- Up to **10% of the monthly salary**.
- At minimum wage: 10% × ₪6,443.85 = **up to ₪644.39/month**.
- If the employee **does not consent**, no food deduction may be made — but then the employee must purchase all their own food and may not use the employer's groceries.

### Step 7.5 — Total Deductions Cap (תקרת ניכויים כוללת)

The **sum of all permitted deductions** (accommodation + utilities + food + medical insurance) **must not exceed 25% of the monthly salary**:

```
Max Total Deductions = 25% × ₪6,443.85 = ₪1,610.96/month
```

If the sum of calculated deductions exceeds this ceiling, reduce proportionally until the 25% cap is met.

## Part 8 — Gross Salary and Net Salary Calculation

### Step 8.1 — Gross Salary (שכר ברוטו)

```
Gross Salary = Base Salary
             + Rest Day Premium (if applicable)
             + Holiday Premium (if applicable)
             + Recovery Pay (if applicable that month)
             − (Sick Day 1 = ₪0; Days 2–3 = 50%; etc., if applicable)
```

> Note: Vacation days are already embedded in the regular salary. Holidays not worked are embedded too. No separate addition is needed for those.

### Step 8.2 — Permitted Deductions (ניכויים מותרים)

```
Total Deductions = Medical Insurance Deduction
                 + Accommodation Deduction
                 + Utilities Deduction
                 + Food Deduction
                 (subject to 25% cap)
```

### Step 8.3 — Income Tax Deduction (ניכוי מס הכנסה)

The employer must withhold income tax (מס הכנסה) from the employee's salary based on:

- The employee's gross taxable salary.
- Credit points (נקודות זיכוי) the employee is entitled to (typically 2.25 for a foreign worker).
- The employer must obtain a withholding tax certificate (תיאום מס / אישור ניכוי מס) from the Tax Authority (רשות המסים).

> ⚠️ At minimum wage, an employee with the standard credit points will generally owe **zero income tax** or a very small amount. Use Israel's official tax brackets (מדרגות מס) to calculate, or use the Tax Authority's online calculator. Do not skip this step — even a ₪0 result must be recorded.

### Step 8.4 — Net Salary (שכר נטו)

```
Net Salary = Gross Salary − Permitted Deductions − Income Tax
```

The net salary is what is actually **paid into the employee's bank account**.

## Part 9 — Employer-Side Payments (תשלומים מהמעסיק — לא ינוכו מהעובד)

These are **additional costs borne entirely by the employer** and must **never be deducted from the employee's salary**. They do not appear in the "deductions" column of the stub but should appear as a memo/employer cost section.

### Step 9.1 — National Insurance (ביטוח לאומי)

- Rate: **3.6% of the salary actually paid to the employee** (effective January 2025).
- Paid by the employer to the National Insurance Institute (ביטוח לאומי) monthly.
- Reported and paid via the NII employer portal or the dedicated phone/online reporting system.
- **The employer cannot deduct this amount from the worker's salary under any circumstances.**

```
Monthly NII Payment = Net Salary Paid × 3.6%
Example: ₪5,000 paid × 3.6% = ₪180/month
```

### Step 9.2 — Pension Substitute / Severance Fund (חלף הפרשות לפנסיה ופיצויים)

Foreign caregiving workers **cannot be enrolled in standard Israeli pension funds** (קרנות פנסיה). Instead, the employer must set aside and pay the employee a **substitute pension deposit** upon termination:

| Component | Rate | From Month |
|-----------|------|-----------|
| Pension substitute (חלף פנסיה) | **6.5%** of monthly salary | Month **7** of employment |
| Severance substitute (חלף פיצויים) | **6%** of monthly salary | Month **7** of employment |
| **Total** | **12.5%** | Month 7+ |

**Key rules:**
- Both components are calculated on **minimum wage** (even if the employer paid more, the baseline is minimum wage — unless actual salary is higher).
- Both are **100% employer-funded** — nothing is deducted from the employee.
- The money is **not transferred monthly** — it accumulates and is paid to the employee **only upon termination of employment** (סיום העסקה).
- The employer must keep track of this accumulating balance on each pay stub as a memo line.

**Monthly accumulation:**
```
Monthly Pension Substitute = 6.5% × ₪6,443.85 = ₪418.85
Monthly Severance Substitute = 6.0% × ₪6,443.85 = ₪386.63
Total Monthly Accrual = ₪805.48
```

> If the employee is dismissed (פוטר) or resigns in circumstances that entitle them to severance, they receive the full accumulated balance. If they resign without entitlement, they still receive the pension substitute portion (6.5%) but may forfeit the severance portion depending on circumstances. Consult an employment attorney for edge cases.

- Seniority in months (ותק בחודשים)

## Part 11 — Full Worked Example (דוגמה מחושבת)

**Scenario:** Month of May 2026. Employee has been working for 10 months. They worked all days, worked one rest day, did not take any vacation, had no sick days, no holiday this month. Employer deducts for food and medical insurance. Employer owns the accommodation.

**Given:**
- Base salary: ₪6,443.85 (minimum wage April 2026)
- Pocket money paid: ₪400 (4 Saturdays)
- Rest days worked: 1
- Seniority: 10 months (no pension/severance yet — starts month 7 ✓ — wait, 10 > 7, so pension applies)
- Medical insurance actual cost: ₪280/month → deductible half = ₪140 (below ₪164.91 cap)
- Food deduction: agreed in contract → 10% × ₪6,443.85 = ₪644.39
- Utilities deduction: ₪94.34 (2026 ceiling)
- No accommodation deduction beyond utilities (employer-owned home, utilities already captured)
- Income tax: ₪0 (at this salary level with standard credit points)

**Step 1 — Gross:**
```
Base Salary:            ₪6,443.85
Pocket money (advance): ₪400.00  ← already paid, shown as advance
Rest Day Premium:       ₪426.35
Gross Total:            ₪6,870.20
```
(Pocket money is part of base, so it's already in ₪6,443.85 — shown separately as a deducted advance at payment. Net salary line will subtract the ₪400 already paid.)

**Step 2 — Deductions:**
```
Medical Insurance:      ₪140.00
Utilities:              ₪94.34
Food:                   ₪644.39
Income Tax:             ₪0.00
Total Deductions:       ₪878.73
878.73 / 6,870.20 = 12.8% → well below 25% cap ✓
```

**Step 3 — Net Pay (remaining bank transfer):**
```
Gross:                  ₪6,870.20
− Deductions:           ₪878.73
= Net:                  ₪5,991.47
− Pocket money paid:    ₪400.00 (already handed over in cash/transfer)
= Bank Transfer:        ₪5,591.47
```

**Step 4 — Employer-Side (not deducted from employee):**
```
NII (3.6% × ₪5,991.47): ₪215.69
Pension accrual (6.5%): ₪418.85
Severance accrual (6%): ₪386.63
Total employer add-on: ₪1,021.17
```

**Step 5 — Leave Balances:**
```
Vacation accrued:  14 ÷ 12 = 1.17 days added
Vacation used:     0
Balance:           10 × 1.17 = 11.7 days cumulative
Sick accrued:      1.5 days
Sick used:         0
Balance:           10 × 1.5 = 15 days cumulative
```

## Part 12 — Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Using outdated minimum wage** — minimum wage updates every April 1st. Always verify at kolzchut.org.il.
2. **Deducting NII from the employee** — it is a 100% employer cost and cannot be passed on.
3. **Calculating pension/severance on the net (post-deduction) salary** — always use the full minimum wage (or actual salary if higher).
4. **Deferring holiday pay to year-end** — each holiday must be paid in the month it occurs.
5. **Forgetting to record pocket money as a salary advance** — it must be line-itemed or the salary calculation will appear short.
6. **Exceeding the 25% deductions cap** — if your deductions add up past 25% of gross, you must reduce them.
7. **Not getting written consent for food/utilities deductions** — verbal agreements do not protect the employer legally.
8. **Starting pension accrual before month 7** — no obligation before month 7, but accrual is mandatory from month 7 onward.
9. **Paying salary in cash without a bank trail** — per Ministry of Finance anti-money-laundering guidelines, salary must be paid by bank transfer or check to the employee's named account.
10. **Not issuing a pay stub at all** — failure to issue a stub (תלוש שכר) is itself a legal violation subject to administrative fines.
